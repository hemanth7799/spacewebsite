NEPTUNE
Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System the third-most-massive planet, and the densest giant planet. Neptune is not visible to the unaided eye and is the only planet in the Solar System found by mathematical prediction rather than by empirical observation. Neptune was subsequently observed with a telescope on 23 September 1846 by Johann Galle . nepptune was visited by Voyager 2, when it flew by the planet on 25 August 1989 Neptune's atmosphere is composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, along with traces of hydrocarbons and possibly nitrogen, but contains a higher proportion of "ices" such as water, ammonia, and methane

TRITON

  1. Triton is named after the Greek sea god Triton,the son of Poseidon
  2. Triton is the largest natural satellite of the planet Neptune.
  3. It was discovered on October 10, 1846, by William Lassell
  4. Triton has a surface of mostly frozen nitrogen
  5. mass      2.14 X 1022kg
  6. temperature      38K

PROTEUS

  1. proteus is the second largest Neptunian moon
  2. it was named after Proteus, the shape-changing sea god of Greek mythology
  3. it was discovered by Voyager 2 spacecraft in 1989
  4. It is shaped more like an irregular polyhedron
  5. mass    4.4 X 1019kg
  6. temperature      51K

GALATEA

  1. Galatea also known as Neptune VI, is the fourth closest satellite of Neptune
  2. It is named after Galatea, one of the Nereids of Greek legend
  3. Galatea was discovered in late July 1989 from the images taken by the Voyager 2
  4. It is irregularly shaped and shows no sign of any geological modification
  5. mass    2.12 X 1018kg
  6. temperature      51K

LARISSA

  1. Larissa is the fifth-closest inner satellite of Neptune
  2. The fourth-largest satellite of Neptune
  3. Larissa is irregular (non-spherical) in shape
  4. Larissa has only been visited by Voyager 2
